## Sprint Retrospective

# What went well
•	Team communication was consistent throughout the Sprint, with regular progress updates and quick resolution of blockers.
•	Customer feedback was collected during the Sprint Review and helped validate the implemented product improvements.
•	Quality assurance was strengthened by adding automated tests, improving CI checks, and updating the project documentation.
•	Sprint tasks were completed according to the Definition of Done before being marked as finished.

# What did not go well
•	Some quality-related tasks required more time than originally estimated, which reduced the time available for feature development.
•	Preparing documentation and collecting all required assignment evidence took longer than expected.
•	A few issues were discovered only during the final testing stage and required additional fixes before release.

# Changes Compared with the Previous Sprint
Based on the previous Sprint Retrospective, the team introduced several improvements:
•	The task allocation format has been improved.
•	Customer feedback was reviewed earlier and used to guide Sprint planning.
•	Testing activities started before the end of the Sprint instead of being postponed until the final days.
•	Documentation was updated continuously during development rather than only before submission.

# Process Improvements for the Next Sprint
•	Introduction of the daily scrum
•	Introduce earlier peer reviews for pull requests to detect issues sooner.
•	Allocate dedicated time for documentation and evidence preparation throughout the Sprint instead of completing everything at the end.

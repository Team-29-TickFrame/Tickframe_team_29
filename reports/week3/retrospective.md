## Sprint Retrospective

## What Went Well

1. MVP v1 core functionality was delivered

The team successfully implemented the core MVP v1 functionality, including candlestick visualization, market metrics calculation, historical OHLCV integration, and the initial analytics interface. This provided a working foundation for future pattern detection and backtesting features.

2. Project documentation and process artifacts were significantly improved

During the Sprint, the team created and maintained key project artifacts, including the user story registry, roadmap, definition of done, changelog, release documentation, and updated README. This improved traceability, transparency, and project organization.

3. Backlog management and workflow became more structured

The Product Backlog was refined into clearly defined PBIs with priorities, story point estimates, MVP version tracking, implementer assignments, reviewer assignments, and acceptance criteria. This improved Sprint planning and progress tracking.

## What Did Not Go Well

1. MVP v1 demonstration materials were not fully available during the Sprint Review

During the customer review meeting, the team was unable to demonstrate all MVP v1 artifacts because some deployment and repository materials were not immediately accessible. This reduced the visibility of completed work.

2. Knowledge distribution across the team needs improvement

Several implementation details were known primarily by individual contributors. As a result, some technical questions during the review could not be answered immediately without consulting the responsible developer.

3. Some planned MVP v1 features remain incomplete

Pattern detection functionality and several advanced analytics features were not fully implemented by the time of the Sprint Review and require additional development and validation.

## Action Points

1. Improve Sprint Review readiness

Before future customer reviews, verify that repositories, deployments, release links, documentation, and demonstration materials are accessible and up to date so that completed work can be reviewed without delays.

2. Increase team-wide visibility of implementation progress

Introduce regular internal synchronization sessions to ensure that multiple team members understand the architecture, implementation status, and current Sprint progress, reducing dependency on individual contributors during reviews and demonstrations.
